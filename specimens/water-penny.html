Water Penny

Sensitivity Rating Sensitive
Phylum Arthropoda
Class Insecta
Order Coleoptera
Family Psephenidae
Genus Sclerocyphon
Size up to 35mm
Habitat Adults: live in vegetation near the stream edge. Females crawl down the edge of rocks to lay their eggs under the water.
Larvae: commonly found in strongly flowing or turbulent sections of a stream, but can survive in stagnant water if it is well oxygenated. Most often they are attached to rocks or large logs.
Diet Larvae: are strictly benthic and feed on periphyton and other plant material covering the stream bed.
Features Adults: are terrestrial. They are board, somewhat flattened and dark coloured. They have distinctively serrated join between their pronotum and elytra.
Larvae: are distinctly flattened and disc-like, oval or sometimes round. Side extensions of every segment form a shield, which completely covers the head and legs of the animal from above. The body of the larvae resembles that of the trilobites
