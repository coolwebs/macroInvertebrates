Non Biting Midge Larva

Sensitivity Rating Very Tolerant
Phylum Arthropoda
Class Insecta
Order Diptera (true flies)
Family Chironomidae
Genus Harrisius and Stenochironomus
Size Up to 30mm
Habitat Sediments of virtually all aquatic habitats; found in large numbers in environments with low oxygen concentrations as well as in healthy environments
Diet Omnivorous, carnivorous or detritivorous depending on species – algae and other debris; animal matter
Features Long, cylindrical and worm-like; visible head; usually a pair of small legs (called prolegs) on first thoracic and last abdominal segment; characteristic figure-of-eight thrashing motion, and backwards motion when attempts are made to pick them up; one group known as bloodworms are light or dark red in colour and are frequently found in large numbers in polluted waters

Gooderham. J. & Tsyrlin. E., (2002)
The waterbug book: a guide to freshwater macroinvertebrates
