Glossary of Terms

  • A-C

  • Abdominal/Abdomen

    One section of the body structure; position varies between organisms.

  • Abiotic

    Component of an environment that is non-living.

  • Algae

    A simple aquatic plant that contains chlorophyll, but lack leaves, roots and vascular tissue.

  • Antennae

    A pair of sensory structures that are used to sense touch, smell, taste and temperature.

  • Benthic

    Bottom of a water body.

  • Biotic

    Component of as environment that is living.

  • Carapace

    Hard dorsal part of an exoskeleton.

  • Carnivorous

    Feeds on animal matter.

  • Copepods

    Small microscopic aquatic crustacean.

  • Crustaceans

    A group of organisms typical covered in a hard shell (yabbies, crabs, shrimp).

  • D-F

  • Detitivorous

    An organism that feeds on and breaks down dead plant and animal matter. They are essential to returning nutrients to
    the ecosystem.

  • Detritus

    Waste from plants or animals.

  • Elytra

    Hard forewings used to protect the membranous hind wings of insects in the order Colepotera.

  • Filter feeding

    Feed by straining suspended matter from water.

  • Gill

    The respiratory organ of aquatic animals, as fish, that breathe oxygen dissolved in water.

  • G-I

  • Herbivorous

    An organism that feeds on plant matter only.

  • Hydrophobic

    Tends to repel or fails to mix with water.

  • Inverterbrate

    An animal lacking a backbone.

  • Larva

    Early stage in the lifecycle of some organisms.

  • M-O

  • Macrophytes

    An aquatic plant that can be seen with the naked eye.

  • Omnivorous

    An organism that feeds on plant and animal matter.

  • Organism/s

    Any living plant, animal or single celled life form (or groups of).

  • Oxygenated

    Enriched with oxygen.

  • P-R

  • Parasitic

    An organism that lives as a parasite (lives on or in another organism and benefits at the hosts expense).

  • Phytoplankton

    Freely floating (often minute) plants that drift with water currents.

  • Predatory

    An organism that hunts, captures and consumes another species.

  • pronotum

    Upper surface of the prothorax (segment of the insect behind the head).

  • Prothorax

    The first part of the thorax of an insect. Usually has the first set of legs attached.

  • Rostrum

    Elongated piercing and sucking mouthparts of insects in the order Hemiptera.

  • S-U

  • Sediment

    Solid particles that settle out of a liquid mixture; minerals weathered or precipitated and accumulate on the beds of waterways.

  • Silt

    Fine sand/clay (sedimentary particles) that are carried by running water, and eventually sink to the bottom of a water body

  • Siphon

    A tubular organ of the respiratory system of some insects that is used as a breathing tube.

  • Species

    A group or organisms capable of breeding and producing fertile offspring.

  • Stagnant

    A body of water that has an unpleasant smell as a result of the lack of water movement.

  • Substrate

    The surface on which an organism grows on, or is attached to.

  • V-Z

  • Tarsi

    Feet of an insect which sometimes ends in claws.

  • Tentacles

    Unsegmented flexible limbs/appendages used to grasp food or assist in moving.

  • Vegetation

    Plants in the environment.

  • Vertebrate

    Groups of animals distinguished through the possession of a backbone/spinal column.

  • Wetlands

    An area of land consisting of marshes and swamps (saturated land).

  • Zooplankton

    Freely floating (often minute) animals that drift with water currents.
